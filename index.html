About

I am a geoscientist interested in the quantitative description of processes that cause petroleum and related fluids to form and migrate in the subsurface. The tools I use are field observations, downhole sampling, chemical analysis, and numerical modelling.

I was a Ph.D. student with Shuhei Ono at MIT, and before that, an undergrad tech for Andre Ellis at Cal State Los Angeles. For my Ph.D., I built instruments and isotopic models to study the production of methane and other reduced gases in sedimentary basins and mantle rocks. I also went to sea to study the ocean carbon cycle [1] and life beneath the seafloor [2].

My current employer is Exxon Mobil Corporation (NYSE:XOM) in Houston, TX. I am responsible for developing geochemical technology and deploying them to multimillion-dollar projects.

Teaching History

Fall 2014: TA for MIT Course 12.104: Introduction to Geochemistry. I designed the laboratory and field components and taught several lectures.
